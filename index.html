🏛️ Organizational Framework & Key Teams

⚙️ Apex Committee

Chairman: Chief Secretary, Government of Sikkim.
Role: Highest decision-making body for effective implementation of all e-Governance schemes, mission mode projects, and strategic IT policies across departments. The committee ensures time-bound execution and inter-departmental synergy.

Members: Secretaries of key depts (HRDD, SJED, Finance), DIT Director, technical experts. Meets quarterly & on-need basis.

👥 Core DIT Team Structure

📌 Secretary (IT) – Administrative head
📌 Director (IT) – Program implementation & e-governance
📌 Joint Director / Deputy Director – Infrastructure, MMPs & capacity building
📌 System Analysts & Programmers – Application development, State Portal, SODAAP data portal
📌 Technical Support Group – Network, OFC coordination, Right of Way management
📌 Training & Capacity Building Cell – Partnering with NIELIT & NISG
📌 District Informatics Officers – e-District services rollout at district HQs.

Total sanctioned strength: approx 45+ technical & administrative personnel, supported by project consultants under NeGP.

🤝 Collaborative Partners & Implementation Agencies

  • NIELIT (National Institute of Electronics & IT) – Skill training in ESDM, digital payments for Group C/D staff, computer literacy programs.
  • NISG (National Institution of Smart Government) – Sponsorship & advanced governance training for officers.
  • Service Providers: BSNL, Reliance Jio, Bharti Airtel, Vodafone Idea – OFC laying, mobile tower infrastructure under Right of Way policy.
  • GoI (MeitY) – NeGP mission mode projects, technical guidance, and funding support for MMPs.
  • State Line Departments: HRDD, SJED, District Administrations for service delivery integration.

⚙️ How DIT Works — End-to-End Operational Model

Core workflow: Policy formulation → Infrastructure enablement (OFC/Towers) → Mission Mode Projects deployment → Citizen services delivery (online & common service centres) → Monitoring via Apex Committee → Feedback & capacity building.

📌 Step 1: Policy & Digital Infrastructure
  • Right of Way (RoW) Policy 2021 (No. 10/DIT/2021, 02/07/2021): grants permissions to telecom operators to lay optical fibre cables and construct towers across Sikkim, reducing digital divide.
  • Connectivity Advocacy: DIT raises issues at high-level GoI meetings, resolves service provider coordination, works towards ‘First Smart State’.
  • Open Data Framework (SODAAP): Sikkim Open Data Acquisition & Dissemination Policy authenticates data to spur human development metrics.
📌 Step 2: Mission Mode Projects (MMPs) & e-District
  • e-District project – Launched e-certificates for SC/ST at District HQ on 09 July 2016; later added 6 other services (income, residence, etc.).
  • State Portal services – Integrated services for HRDD (education dept), SJED (social justice).
  • Citizen outreach – Doorstep delivery under NeGP objective.
  • Services available via Common Service Centres (CSCs) and district e-service counters.
📌 Step 3: Flagship — Cyber Village Model

Pilot: 23-Melli Dara Paiyong Gram Panchayat Unit (GPU) → India’s 1st successfully implemented Cyber Village (award-winning).
How it works: End-to-end digital literacy, broadband access, e-governance kiosk, digital payment adoption, online government services at Gram Panchayat level.
Next Phase: Scale to 100 additional GPUs across Sikkim, replicating the model with upgraded infrastructure and community training.

📌 Step 4: Open Government Data Portal

sikkim.data.gov.in launched 10th June 2016 – Sikkim is the FIRST state in India to operationalize an Open Government Data portal under SODAAP policy.
Function: Provides authentic, real-time datasets on key development metrics (health, education, economy). Used by policymakers, researchers, citizens for transparency and evidence-based planning.

📱 Digital Service Delivery Channels

  • ✅ Tourist Information Mobile App – Available on Android, Windows, iOS platforms for real-time tourism updates, destinations, e-help.
  • ✅ State Portal Integration – Single-window for department-specific citizen services.
  • ✅ Digital Payments Training – DIT + NIELIT conduct workshops for Group C & D staff on cashless governance.

🏅 Landmark Achievements & Accolades

🏆 India’s First Cyber Village (Melli Dara Paiyong GPU)

Recognized at highest levels (National awards for e-governance). The model has been studied by other states. Transformational impact: digital inclusion in remote rural areas, direct benefit transfer awareness, paperless gram panchayat operations.

📊 First State Open Data Portal (SODAAP)

Pioneer in open data dissemination — sikkim.data.gov.in. Sets benchmark for transparency & public data access. Received appreciation from Government of India and NITI Aayog.

🚀 e-District & e-Certificate Initiative

Launch of SC/ST e-certificates at District Headquarters (July 9, 2016) drastically reduced turnaround time, eliminated physical paperwork, and improved accessibility for marginalized communities. Subsequently added six more services, serving thousands of citizens annually.

📡 RoW Policy & Telecom Leadership

Sikkim DIT formulated the first comprehensive Right of Way policy in the region, enabling rapid optical fibre rollout. Active resolution of connectivity blackspots through periodic service provider meets – directly improving mobile & broadband penetration.

📚 Human Resource Development & Digital Literacy

Bridging the digital divide – systematic training verticals:

🎓 Government Employee Training
  • ✔ Basic computer training (regular batches)
  • ✔ Advanced IT & skill enhancement programs
  • ✔ Sponsorship to NISG, GoI training courses
  • ✔ e-Government & Digital Payments workshop (Group C & D staff) – in association with NIELIT.
💻 ESDM Skill Development

In collaboration with NIELIT, DIT conducts skill training programmes in Electronic System Design and Manufacturing (ESDM) discipline to create local talent pool for electronics & IT hardware ecosystem.

Special focus: Rural youth, women, and candidates from remote GPUs to ensure inclusive growth.

📈 Digital Village Champions

Under Cyber Village expansion plan, each GPU will have trained "Digital Saksharta" volunteers & gram panchayat tech coordinators. DIT provides continuous handholding, monthly refresher training, and support for service delivery.

📜 Policies & Strategic Documentation

📌 Sikkim Open Data Acquisition & Dissemination Policy (SODAAP)

Notified to ensure authentic, machine-readable datasets. The policy defines data standards, responsible authorities, periodic data updation cycles, and public access mechanisms. Powered by sikkim.data.gov.in.

📌 Right of Way Policy 2021 (No. 10/DIT/2021, 02/07/2021)

Streamlines permissions for OFC laying & tower erection – single window clearance, nominal fees, faster approvals to telecom operators, boosting 4G/5G readiness and BharatNet connectivity.

📌 IT Policy of Government of Sikkim (1999)

Gazette notification no. GO/3/DI/99 dated 31.03.1999 – the foundational document that led to establishment of DIT in 2000. Focus areas: e-governance, digital infrastructure, citizen-centric services.

📌 NeGP Alignment & State MMPs

All Mission Mode Projects (Income Tax, MCA21, e-District, etc.) are executed under National e-Governance Plan guidelines with state-specific customizations.

📡 Telecom & Connectivity — Operational Mechanism

🔧 Problem-Solving Approach

DIT proactively addresses connectivity challenges by:
➜ Organizing high-level meets with DoT, GoI.
➜ Regular review meetings with BSNL, Jio, Airtel, Vodafone.
➜ Field-level coordination for last-mile connectivity in difficult terrains.
➜ Leveraging RoW policy to fast-track permissions – ensuring optical fibre network expansion.

📡 Goal: First 'Smart State' of India

Target seamless digital infrastructure – robust telecom backbone, universal broadband access, and high-speed connectivity across all 6 districts. DIT acts as nodal department liaising with central schemes like BharatNet, USOF.

Current coverage: Major towns & rural clusters connected via OFC; Mobile tower density increased by 35% post-RoW policy implementation. Over 500+ km OFC laid in collaboration with private operators (2022–24).

🚀 Vision Ahead — What DIT is Executing Next

🌾 Cyber Village Expansion (100 GPUs)

Based on success of 23-Melli Dara Paiyong GPU, DIT plans to replicate the Cyber Village model in 100 additional Gram Panchayat Units. Each Cyber Village will include WiFi hotspots, digital literacy centre, e-district service kiosk, and cashless transaction training.

📱 New MMPs & Service Delivery

Integration of additional citizen-facing services (revenue, labour, public grievances) under e-District 2.0. Mobile app enhancements for tourism and agriculture advisories.

🧠 AI & Data Analytics

DIT exploring integration of AI-driven insights from Open Government Data portal to support evidence-based policy making for health, education, and tourism sectors. Upcoming Centre of Excellence in Data Analytics.

🤝 NIELIT ESDM Expansion

Scaling up electronics manufacturing training to meet industry demands, generating employment in Sikkim’s technology sector.

🔁 How the DIT Ecosystem Works – Integrated Summary

➤ Policy & Planning Wing: drafts IT policies (SODAAP, RoW) & e-governance roadmap.
➤ Infrastructure Team: coordinates with telecom providers, OFC rollout, tower approvals.
➤ e-Governance & MMP Cell: implements e-District, State Portal, cyber village projects.
➤ Open Data Division: manages Sikkim Open Government Data portal (sikkim.data.gov.in) – first in India.
➤ Capacity Building Unit: runs training for govt employees, Group C/D staff digital payments, ESDM skilling through NIELIT.
➤ Monitoring & Evaluation: Apex Committee chaired by Chief Secretary reviews progress, ensures time-bound delivery of citizen services.
➤ Result: Transparent, accountable, digitally inclusive governance — recognized with national awards and 'First Smart State' vision.

✅ Every component works in synergy — from Right of Way policy enabling connectivity to the last Cyber Village delivering SC/ST certificates at doorstep.
